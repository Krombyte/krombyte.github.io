The Spirit of American Muscle

American muscle cars are icons of power, style, and rebellion. Born in the 1960s, they became legends for combining raw horsepower with aggressive looks and affordability. Today, they continue to evolve with modern engineering while staying true to their roots.

Famous Muscle Car Facts

  • The term “muscle car” was first used in the mid-1960s to describe affordable American coupes with high-performance V8 engines.
  • The 1970s oil crisis temporarily ended the golden age of muscle cars due to stricter emissions and fuel economy rules.
  • Modern models, like the Charger Hellcat and Mustang GT500, exceed 700 horsepower — something unimaginable decades ago.
  • Muscle cars are celebrated globally as symbols of freedom and American automotive culture.
